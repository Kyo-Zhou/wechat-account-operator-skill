# Workflow Details

Use this reference when the task spans more than one stage.

## A. Topic Discovery

Pick topics that satisfy at least two of these:

- near-term timing
- clear policy or regulatory driver
- direct impact on policyholders, buyers, or practitioners
- room for interpretation instead of simple news summary

Good examples:

- product rule changes
- demonstration rate changes
- solvency and governance developments
- listed insurer results with market implications
- company restructurings that raise consumer-protection questions

Do not assume the account is insurance-focused.

If the user gives a custom direction, rewrite the filter in that context.

Examples:

- AI: model launches, pricing shifts, product releases, benchmark gaps, workflow changes
- education: policy changes, exam trends, learning methods, institution strategy
- retail: category shifts, channel changes, pricing pressure, consumer behavior
- legal: policy interpretation, compliance points, case-driven explainers

## B. Positioning The Piece

Turn the article into one sentence before drafting:

- "This article explains why X changed and what Y should do next."

If that sentence is weak, the article usually is too.

## C. Drafting Order

Write in this sequence:

1. title candidate
2. opening hook
3. 3 to 5 section headers
4. body under each section
5. closing paragraph
6. digest

This prevents drifting into a shapeless long article.

## D. Editing Priorities

Cut first:

- repeated regulatory phrasing
- abstract statements with no reader value
- stacked rhetorical questions
- AI-sounding transitions

Keep:

- sharp claims
- concrete deadlines
- direct reader implications
- useful framing sentences

## E. Post-Publish Review

After the draft is uploaded, note:

- what in the opening worked
- where the article still felt too long
- which layout modules felt native to WeChat
- which design choices felt too tool-like
